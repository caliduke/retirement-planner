2026 Retirement Rules

1. The 403b Advantage

Every dollar entered in 403b Contrib is "hidden" from the tax man. It lowers your Federal Tax, your CA Tax, and most importantly, your MAGI (the number Medicare uses to set your premiums).

2. Social Security "Tax Torpedo"

The IRS taxes up to 85% of your Social Security if your other income is high. However, California (CA) taxes 0%. This is why you'll notice your Federal tax bill grow faster than your CA bill as you increase withdrawals.

3. The 12% Bracket Goal

Your goal is to "fill" the 12% federal tax bracket. For a couple over 65, this usually means keeping your Federal Taxable Income (visible in your spreadsheet) under $100,800. Once you pass this, your tax rate jumps to 22%.

4. IRMAA & The $218k Cliff

Medicare looks at your MAGI from two years ago. If you cross $218,000 (for a couple), you hit the first surcharge cliff. This adds roughly $1,944/year in extra premiums. Use Roth/HSA withdrawals to stay under this line.

5. The RRSP "Shadow" Growth

California is unique: they tax the internal interest/dividends inside a Canadian RRSP even if you don't withdraw it. Enter that growth in the RRSP Growth field to see the impact on your CA bill only.
